What is mathscon?

Mathscon is a science conference directed at young researchers, PhD students and undergraduates interested in mathematics and its related fields. Event aims to expose the public to different activities to make them realize how truly amazing Mathematics is. Renowned speakers will give inspirational lectures. Intellectual workshops will be organised in order to illustrate the applications of the mother of all sciences. And finally the conference will end with an engaging social event during which, one will have the chance to discuss anything with the organisers, speakers and other guests.


Why mathscon?

We are a group of a students who admire the beauty of Mathematics. Mathematics is about the concept, not the calculations. It is about shaping thought, not following rules. And we want everyone to know about this. Our mission is to reshape the world's perception of Mathematics.

As we all are subject enthusiasts, we are also sharing the experience of doing research, learning similar modules or stumbling upon hard questions in Mathematics. In an attempt to collaborate and find synergy from our knowledge around the World in a single place we invite you to London – a place you will surely fit in!

Event Segments

Keynote Speakers

Throughout the event, you will have the chance to experience an extended TEDx style talk, from top researchers and professionals across the field of Mathematics. A Field medalist, mathemagician and others are expected to join us!

Panel Discussions

To change the way Mathematics is perceived, we will also be presenting topics for the audience to engage in through panel discussions. We plan to host 3 different discussions, and these will range from traditional questions that have troubled scientists for centuries, to the latest hot topics surrounding recent developments and new applications of Mathematics.

Workshops

For many, Mathematics might be hard to understand without a pen and paper approach or a specific problem to solve. That's why we are introducing workshops. Have you ever wondered what the numbers behind a Rubix cube are? Wanted to challenge a chess Grandmaster? What about the probabilities in poker? These are just a few more reasons to join us!

Networking

Whether you want to pursue an academic career in research, or explore a professional mathematical career in the workplace, one thing that is certain is that networking with people who have common interests with you is the key behind success.

About Blackett


The conference will take place at Imperial College London, on 11th of February. The venue for the event will be the Blackett Building with space for workshops within the same amenity. Providing capacity for over 250 students, expect great questions and interesting answers. After a full day at Imperial, we will head out to our social part of the event - mathscon ball. The location of that part is secret and yet to be revealed.
